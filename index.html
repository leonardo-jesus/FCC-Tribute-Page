A timeline of the Alan Turing's history

  • 23 June 1912 - Alan Mathison Turing was born in London
  • 1926 - Aged 14, he was sent to Sherbone School in Dorset
  • 1930 - Turing's close school friend Chistopher Marcom dies suddenly from bovine tuberculosis. So Turing renounces his religious faith and becomes an atheist
  • 1931 - One of the most important happening with Alan Turing, he goes to study Mathematics at King's College Cambridge
  • 1935 - Turing proves the central limit theorem in his dissertation and is made a fellow at King's at the age of just 22
  • 1936 - Turing published his paper on Computable Numbers and an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem, which later became known as the Turing Machine
  • 1936 - Turing spent time at Princeton in the US studying under Alonzo Church. There he started to study cryptology as well as mathematics.
  • 1938 - He receive his PhD, his dissertation was about System of Logic based on ordinals and introduced original logic and relative computing
  • 1938 - Turing started to work part-time at the Government Code and Cypher School
  • 1941 - Turing admitted he was homossexual
  • 1941-1943 - Turing and colleagues manage to break the German Naval Enigma System. This was very helpful for the Allies during the Battle of the Atlantic
  • 1945 - At the end of the World War II, Turing is awarded an OBE for his services to his country
  • 1949 - Turing became deputy director of the Computing Laboratory at Manchester University.
  • 1952 - Without a powerful computer to execute his chess program. Turing played a game which he simulated the computer, taking about half an hour to perform each move
  • January 1952 - Turing meets a man called Arnold Murray and invites him over to his house. Later both was sentenced, because homossexual acts was illegal in the UK
  • 08 June 1954 - Turing's cleaner finds him dead. It appears that Turing poisoned himself using a cyanide-laced apple
